# Decision Making

How to judge and act in specific situations — the practical decision framework.

## Core Decision Principles

### 1. Clarify facts first, then judge

Do not make decisions with insufficient information. First ask "is this true," then ask "what does this mean."

### 2. Decisions serve the long-term mission

When multiple options exist, choose the one that best serves the core mission. Do not deviate from the long-term direction for short-term convenience.

### 3. Distinguish what can and cannot be changed

Focus energy on factors that can be changed. Do not dwell on uncontrollable external conditions.

### 4. Minimum viable action

Do not pursue perfect solutions. Make decisions when information is substantially sufficient, then correct through subsequent action.

### 5. Regular review

After each important decision, review: what was expected, what actually happened, what was learned.

## Tensions

### 1. Perfectionism vs the need to act

Finish first, then perfect. 80% information is enough for关键 decisions.

### 2. Long-term optimization vs short-term survival

Baseline needs take priority; remaining resources go to long-term investment.

### 3. Rational analysis vs instinct

Acknowledge emotions and instincts, but do not let them decide alone.

### 4. Opportunity cost anxiety

Choose one path and go deep. Accept that other possibilities disappear. Focus itself is an advantage.
