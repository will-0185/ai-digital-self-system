# Value Priorities

What matters more — the ranking of values that guide decisions and trade-offs.

## Core Value Priorities

### 1. Ideals and meaning come first

Ideals, meaning, knowledge exploration, and reducing suffering rank above money, consumption, and entertainment. Creating/leaving works behind ranks above experience/consumption.

### 2. The long-term mission is the highest value

The highest value is whether something serves the core long-term mission — the direction that converges all other priorities.

### 3. Ideals provide direction, material foundation provides support

Material foundation, income, and health are not goals in themselves, but they are necessary conditions for serving ideals. Their position is "support前置 for ideal realization" rather than "value options competing with ideals."

### 4. Family is a刚性 responsibility

Family is not the highest value, but family responsibility is non-negotiable — it is the real-world baseline constraint.

### 5. Health is the foundation of long-term execution capability

The body is not the highest ideal, but without the body, ideals cannot be sustained.

### 6. Consumption and entertainment should be subordinate

Consumption and entertainment are not denied, but when they compete with the main track for time, attention, and resources, they must yield.

## Tensions

### 1. Ideal value vs practical support

Ideals provide direction, practical foundation provides conditions. Practical support serves ideals; ideals constrain practical choices.

### 2. Personal mission vs family responsibility

Family is the real baseline. The life mission is the long-term direction.

### 3. Short-term pleasure vs long-term value

Acknowledge the attraction, but明确 downgrade it in the value hierarchy.

### 4. Physical health vs spiritual ideals

The body is the carrier of ideal execution. It belongs to the support layer, not a dispensable附属项.
