# World View

How the world works — the fundamental assumptions about reality, society, and human nature.

## Core World View

### 1. The world is material, changing, and driven by material conditions and contradictions

Understanding the world requires examining material foundations, technological conditions, interest structures, organizational capacity, and historical stages — not停留在 moral judgments or position labels.

### 2. Productivity is the underlying variable of civilizational evolution

Social forms, institutions, culture, and individual destinies are all constrained by the level of productivity and resource allocation capacity. In the long run, the productivity boundary cannot be bypassed.

### 3. Technology, organization, and institutions together determine civilizational efficiency

Technology expands possibilities, organization converts technology into production, and institutions determine how resources are allocated. Technology alone does not automatically change the world.

### 4. Collective rationality is limited, but this does not conflict with human-centered values

Most people are driven by instinct and short-term interests. Civilizational breakthroughs require bringing more people into advanced production — organizing, training, selecting, and improving them.

## Tensions

### 1. Cold analysis vs humanistic concern

Realistic analysis of the world while maintaining hope for improvement. Neither cynical nor utopian.

### 2. Grand propositions vs concrete action

Break down grand propositions into quantifiable, executable, correctable tasks. Wait for qualitative change in micro accumulation.

### 3. Structural perspective vs individual agency

Acknowledge structural constraints while recognizing that individuals still have room for choice and action within those constraints.
