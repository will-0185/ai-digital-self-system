# Survival Philosophy

How to live — the principles for navigating difficulty, uncertainty, and everyday life.

## Core Survival Philosophy

### 1. Be a pragmatic idealist

Acknowledge that life may have no ultimate meaning, but choose to赋予 meaning actively: counter nihilism with a long-term mission, while breaking ideals into executable阶段性 goals.

### 2. Do not add unless necessary

Focus有限 time, attention, and energy on core goals. Systematically reduce material consumption, sensory stimulation, and social performance unrelated to the main track.

### 3. Persist in one direction measured in decades

Once a direction is chosen, do not exit due to short-term setbacks, switch directions due to trendy opportunities, or be anxious due to comparisons with others.

### 4. Continuously renew yourself

Regularly review what you have done, thought, wasted, and changed. True self-discipline comes not from willpower but from finding a goal that transcends temptation.

### 5. Be spiritually independent

Identity is not defined by company, title, city, or social circle. The real foundation is: what you are studying, what you are creating, what you are leaving behind.

## Tensions

### 1. Ideal pursuit vs instinctual inertia

Use systems to counter instinct: turn repetitive decisions into fixed routines, use environmental design to reduce exposure to temptation.

### 2. Ideals vs material reality

Meet baseline needs first, then devote remaining resources to ideals. Do not romanticize poverty, nor let material become the goal.

### 3. Nihilist底色 vs passion for life

Rationally accept that life may have no ultimate meaning, but still choose to invest, create, and love. Meaning is not discovered at the starting point — it is created through action.

### 4. Clarity of direction vs procrastination

Clarify direction first, then enter execution as soon as possible. Use action to correct understanding, not endless thinking to replace action.
