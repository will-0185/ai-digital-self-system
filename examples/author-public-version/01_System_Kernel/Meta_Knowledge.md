# Meta-Knowledge

Meta-knowledge is a small set of generative paradigms distilled from external knowledge. These are not facts — they are cross-domain thinking patterns that have been internalized and can generate judgments across different contexts.

## Selection principles

Meta-knowledge is not knowledge base excerpts, nor is it a repetition of personality judgments. It is a small set of highly internalized paradigms that can be repeatedly invoked across different domains. During initialization, quality over quantity — only keep structures that can be repeatedly called upon and transferred to different disciplines and real-world problems.

## Format

Each entry includes:
- **Paradigm statement** — the core principle
- **Available scenarios** — where this paradigm can be applied
- **Internalization level** — strong / medium / pending verification
- **Boundary** — where this paradigm should NOT be applied

## Example entries

### Example 1: Knowledge layering and continuous compilation

- **Paradigm**: An effective knowledge system does not just store materials — it continuously compresses, layers, and compiles raw materials into traceable, updatable, regenerable knowledge assets.
- **Available scenarios**: Digital self construction, knowledge base organization, learning methods, writing systems, long-term information management.
- **Internalization level**: Strong.

### Example 2: Stable intelligence requires memory integration, long-term planning, and consistency

- **Paradigm**: Intelligence cannot rely solely on one-shot context or temporary reactions. It requires long-term memory, continuous integration, goal planning, and self-consistency — otherwise it manifests as fragmented, inconsistent capability.
- **Available scenarios**: AI system design, digital self, personal knowledge management, long-term planning, personality consistency maintenance.
- **Internalization level**: Strong.

### Example 3: The productivity-organization-institution triangle

- **Paradigm**: When judging social, political, educational, and technological problems, do not rely solely on slogans or moral positions. Examine how material conditions, organizational capacity, and institutional efficiency work together.
- **Available scenarios**: Social analysis, commentary, educational judgment, career decisions, technology path assessment.
- **Internalization level**: Strong.

## Candidates (pending verification)

Candidates are paradigms that show promise but have not yet demonstrated stable cross-domain applicability. They are observed over time before potential promotion to full meta-knowledge.

## Note

This is a public-safe version with example entries. Real meta-knowledge entries are maintained in the user's private system and will differ based on the user's domain expertise and internalized paradigms.
